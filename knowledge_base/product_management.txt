ROLE: Product Manager / Associate Product Manager / Technical Product Manager
DOMAIN: Product Strategy, UX, Roadmapping, Stakeholder Management

=== COMMON INTERVIEW TOPICS ===
1. Product Sense
   - Defining and measuring success metrics (KPIs, OKRs, NPS, DAU/MAU)
   - Product critique: identifying user pain points and opportunities
   - Prioritization frameworks: RICE, MoSCoW, ICE, Kano model
   - Feature ideation and product roadmapping

2. Analytical Thinking
   - Root cause analysis: how to investigate metric drops
   - Data-driven decision making
   - A/B testing: design, minimum detectable effect, sample size
   - Funnel analysis, cohort analysis, retention analysis

3. Strategy & Market
   - Competitive analysis (Porter's Five Forces, SWOT)
   - Go-to-market strategy
   - Business model thinking
   - Identifying target customers (personas, jobs-to-be-done)

4. Execution & Cross-functional
   - Working with Engineering, Design, Marketing
   - Writing PRDs (Product Requirements Documents)
   - Managing ambiguity and trade-offs
   - Agile/Scrum ceremonies and PM's role

5. Technical Depth (for Technical PM)
   - APIs, databases, system architecture basics
   - Understanding technical constraints and feasibility

=== SAMPLE INTERVIEW QUESTIONS ===
PRODUCT SENSE:
Q: Design a feature for Google Maps that helps elderly users.
Q: How would you improve LinkedIn's job recommendation feature?
Q: Your DAU drops 20% on Monday. Walk me through how you investigate.
Q: How would you prioritize a backlog of 50 feature requests?
Q: Design an alarm clock app from scratch.

ANALYTICAL:
Q: How would you measure the success of Instagram Stories?
Q: Define success metrics for a food delivery app's new subscription plan.
Q: How do you decide between two competing feature ideas?

BEHAVIORAL:
Q: Tell me about a product you shipped that failed. What did you learn?
Q: How do you manage disagreements between Engineering and Design?
Q: Describe a time you had to make a decision with incomplete data.

=== MODEL ANSWERS (CIRCLES / STAR FRAMEWORK) ===
For "Design a feature" questions use CIRCLES:
C - Comprehend the situation (clarify scope)
I - Identify the customer
R - Report customer needs
C - Cut through prioritization
L - List solutions
E - Evaluate trade-offs
S - Summarize recommendation

For "DAU dropped 20%":
1. Check for data pipeline issues first
2. Segment: iOS vs Android, geographies, user cohorts
3. Check for external factors: competitor launch, news event
4. Identify which funnel step dropped: acquisition, activation, retention
5. Form hypotheses and validate with data

=== PREPARATION TIPS ===
1. Use real products you use daily — have opinions and metrics ready
2. Practice frameworks but apply them flexibly, not rigidly
3. Always clarify before diving into an answer — interviewers reward structured thinking
4. Know 3–5 products deeply: their metrics, user segments, competitive landscape
5. Read: "Inspired" by Marty Cagan, "The Lean Startup" by Eric Ries
6. Practice estimation questions (market sizing) out loud
7. Build a portfolio of side projects or case studies
8. Prepare for SQL / Excel / basic analytics questions at many companies

=== SKILLS TO HIGHLIGHT ===
- User empathy and design thinking
- Data analysis and metric definition
- Stakeholder communication and alignment
- Roadmap creation and prioritization
- Agile methodology and sprint planning
- Customer research and usability testing
