CATEGORY: Career Guidance, Professional Development, Job Search Strategy

=== JOB SEARCH STRATEGY ===
1. Define Your Target
   - Identify 3 target roles (specific job titles, not broad categories)
   - Identify 10–15 target companies per batch
   - Know the difference: startup, scale-up, enterprise, consulting

2. Resume & Application
   - Tailor resume for each application (ATS keyword matching)
   - Keep to 1 page for < 5 years experience; 2 pages for senior roles
   - Lead with a strong summary section (3–4 lines)
   - Use action verbs + metric (increased, reduced, built, launched + %)
   - ATS-friendly format: no tables, no images, standard fonts, PDF/DOCX

3. Networking (Accounts for ~70% of hires)
   - LinkedIn: optimize headline, about section, and featured section
   - Informational interviews: reach out to 5 people/week in target companies
   - Template: "Hi [Name], I admire your work at [Company]. I'm exploring [role] and would love 20 minutes of your time to learn from your experience."
   - Alumni networks, college events, professional communities

4. Interview Pipeline Management
   - Track all applications in a spreadsheet (company, role, status, contact, date)
   - Follow up after 7–10 business days if no response
   - Prepare company-specific answers for each interview

=== LEARNING ROADMAP BY EXPERIENCE ===
FRESHER / ENTRY LEVEL (0–1 year):
- Focus: Build foundational skills + 2–3 showcase projects
- Certifications: IBM SkillsBuild, Google, Coursera, AWS Cloud Practitioner
- Portfolio: GitHub with 3 quality projects, a personal website/LinkedIn
- Target: internships, junior roles, graduate programs

MID-LEVEL (2–5 years):
- Focus: Depth in a domain + cross-functional skills
- Lead a project, mentor juniors, take ownership of a module
- Pursue 1–2 advanced certifications
- Contribute to open source, write technical articles

SENIOR / LEAD (5+ years):
- Focus: Strategic thinking, architecture, influence
- Build a personal brand (speaking, writing, community)
- Develop leadership and people management skills
- Consider MBA, executive programs, or advanced degrees if targeting management

=== LINKEDIN OPTIMIZATION TIPS ===
1. Professional headshot (increases profile views 14x)
2. Headline: [Title] | [Skill] | [Value] (not just job title)
   Example: "Data Science Student | Python & ML | Building AI for Social Good"
3. About section: 3 paragraphs — who you are, what you do, what you're seeking
4. Feature section: link to your top project, article, or portfolio
5. Skills section: 5–10 relevant skills, get endorsements
6. Recommendations: ask 1–2 colleagues or professors for written recommendations
7. Engage: comment thoughtfully on posts in your field (2x/week minimum)
8. Post: share a project, learning, or insight monthly

=== SALARY NEGOTIATION ===
1. Always negotiate — 85% of employers expect it
2. Research: Glassdoor, Levels.fyi, LinkedIn Salary, AmbitionBox (India)
3. Strategy: Let them give the first number; counter 10–15% above target
4. Never accept on the spot: "I'm very excited. Can I have 24–48 hours to review?"
5. Negotiate the full package: base, bonus, equity, benefits, WFH policy, learning budget
6. Use competing offers as leverage (only if real)
